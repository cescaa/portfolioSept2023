BiteClub


(ADOBE / FIGMA / NEXT.JS / REACT / TAILWIND)

As part of the PRJ566 and PRJ666 courses at Seneca, my team designed and developed a food exploration web application called BiteClub within 8 months. The application aims to help Gen Z and Millennials feel less anxious about choosing what to eat, discover Toronto’s diverse food cultures, and encourage picky eaters to step outside their comfort zones.

Key Features
  • Restaurant profiles and reviews
  • AI-assisted restaurant recommendations
  • AI-powered food challenges
  • User-generated blogs
  • General user profiles
  • Search functionality for restaurants, blogs, and users

As the lead UX/UI Designer, I converted my teammates’ low-fidelity grayscale wireframes into high-fidelity, full-colour mockups, ensuring that our designs aligned with UX/UI principles and standards. I also designed our logo and customized all graphics using Adobe Illustrator. During development, I created most of the front-end components.

Project Timeline and Constraints

While PRJ566 and PRJ666 ran for eight months, competing course requirements, such as business proposals and data/process models, reduced our development window. Ultimately, we had only four weeks to design both grayscale and high-fidelity mockups, and twelve weeks to build the entire application.
Due to the limited timeframe, and because neither course focused on UX/UI, we were unable to conduct primary user research and testing, nor design multiple iterations of our wireframes and prototypes.

Our Process and Collaboration

To develop BiteClub’s colour palette and brand identity, I asked my team members to create a moodboard. This would allow us to explore everyone’s ideas and narrow down the visual direction together.

Olha's moodboard

Nicole's moodboard

Irish's moodboard

While my teammates created their moodboards, I refined their low-fidelity grayscale wireframes into higher-fidelity versions. After they finished, I applied the colours to these polished designs, which guided our team in finalizing the palette.

We experimented with different colour palattes, and chose Nicole's palatte of greens, yellows, blues, and peaches (BOTTOM LEFT).

We chose Nicole’s palette of bright greens, yellows, blues, and peaches because we wanted our application to feel vibrant, playful, and inviting, reflecting the fun, exploratory spirit of BiteClub. These colours were also reminiscent of fresh fruits and vegetables, such as citrus and leafy greens, which we felt was especially fitting. We also used this time to decide on font styles. Similarly, we chose styles that were fun, playful, and inviting.

Once we decided on the colour palette and font styles, I applied them to the polished grayscale wireframes to create the high-fidelity mockups.
