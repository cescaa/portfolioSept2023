My team's design for Seneca Software Developers Club's (SSDC) homepage.

My team designed and developed a website for Seneca Software Developers Club (SSDC). Our project won Best Overall Hack and Most Creative Hack for Design Hacks 2024.

Challenge Details
  1. Design and develop a website that reflects SSDC's vision and goals
  2. Align with the club’s colour palette, logo, and theme
  3. Must be responsive, scalable, and ready for implementation
  4. Foster a sense of community and collaboration among Seneca's software developers
  5. Complete within 1 week

We ensured our design aligned with SSDC's existing logo.

Our Process

I designed a few iterations of the website, but due to time constraints, my exploration was limited. Irish began development while I designed on Figma. When the final mockups were complete, I assited with development. Although our brand colours were predetermined, I still had to make many design decisions, such as defining the typography and graphic elements.

Design Approach

To meet the challenge requirements, I designed the site to express the club’s values: community and inclusivity, and growth and innovation. Animation grabs the users' attention, but also reflects the different moving parts of a software program. I used boxy, geometric typography to echo code blocks and hardware, giving the brand a "techy" feel. However, it's very easy to make tech-related websites feel cold and impersonal, so I avoided harsh lines and used animations and cartoon-like graphics to add warmth.
