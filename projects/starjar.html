StarJar
(WEB530 Winner)


(ADOBE / FIGMA / REACT NATIVE)

StarJar is an app helps kids (8-14 years-old) prepare for adulthood by encouraging them to complete chores to earn “stars”, which can be redeemed for rewards. Their guardians can create custom chores and rewards, while kids choose to either save their stars or spend them right away.

Challenge Details

For Seneca’s WEB530 (Cross-Platform App Development), I and my three team members were challenged to design and develop an original app in just five days. A panel of industry professionals selected our project, StarJar, as the best app among seven teams at the end of the course. The challenge requirements were the following:

  1. Ideate, design, and develop an original app within 5 days
  2. Create a logo for your app
  3. Develop your app using React Native
  4. Present your finished app to a panel of industry professionals

Our Process

After our group finalized our idea, Cian and I designed StarJar’s interface in Figma. Our system uses bright, high-contrast colours to provide our young users' visual stimulation for their developing eyes, attract their attention, and encourage exploration. The large, rounded, highly legible text improves readability, and generous tap targets make actions easy for young users. Because the timeline was so tight, the whole team began development while Cian and I were still designing. We finished our app on time with only minor bugs remaining.
