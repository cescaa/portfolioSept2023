Where Are You?


(HTML / CSS / JS / JQUERY)

The main website's first chapter; Sections are unlocked for viewing purposes.
Scroll over image to see full page.

My team of three and I developed an interactive transmedia narrative: a story told across various media platforms and artifacts, including a website, a chat simulation, social media, and even 3D models. Our primary storytelling hub was a website, where users had the challenge of unlocking chapters and chapter sections by answering questions. These answers were hidden within the other media platforms and artifacts, requiring users to seek them out. But of course, to make our narrative more fun and engaging, we added red herrings to divert them away from the correct answers. Once users successfully answered all the questions, they were rewarded with the gratifying conclusion of our story.

The Rabbit Hole

The pandemic forced us to adapt our rabbit hole: our method of getting our audience to find and experience our transmedia narrative. The user would have to scan the QR code on the back of a physical photo, which direct them to the main website. If not for the pandemic, we would have printed multiple copies of the photo and hid them around our school campus for people to find.
To present it to our instructor, I created a rotating 3D model on Blender and displayed it on Unity. The button beneath the photo is linked to a 2D image of the QR code.
